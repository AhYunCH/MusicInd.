Taylor Swift

Taylor Alison Swift(born December 13, 1989) is an American singer-songwriter, record producer, actress, music video director, philanthropist and businesswoman. In 2006, she released her debut single "Tim McGraw", then her self-titled debut album, which was subsequently certified multi-platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America.

In November 2008, Swift released her second album, Fearless, and the recording earned Taylor four Grammy Awards, including the Grammy Award for Album of the Year, at the 52nd Grammy Awards.Swift released her third album Speak Now on October 25, 2010, which sold 1,047,000 copies in its first week. Swift's fourth studio album, Red, was released in October 2012. She wrote nine of the album's sixteen songs alone.Taylor has released a new pop album which is entitled "1989" with popular hits like "Shake It Off" , "Blank Space", "Style", "Bad Blood", and "Wildest Dream". There were over 1 million copies sold on the first week of its release date.

In August 2017, Swift successfully sued David Mueller, a former morning show personality for Denver's KYGO-FM.Swift thereafter cleared her social media accounts and then released "Look What You Made Me Do" as the lead single from her sixth album, Reputation. She then released her sixth studio album "Reputation" on November 10,2017. Swift released "Me!", featuring Brendon Urie of Panic! at the Disco on April 26, 2019, as the lead single from her seventh studio album,Lover,released on August 23, 2019 and debuted atop the Billboard 200 with first-week sales of 679,000 copies. Upon the album's release, all of its 18 songs entered the Billboard Hot 100, setting the record for the most simultaneous chart entries for a female artist.

Swift's eighth studio album, Folklore, was a surprise album released on July 24, 2020. Written and recorded while Swift was in isolation during the COVID-19 pandemic.Swift's ninth studio album, Evermore, was released on December 11, 2020, along with its leading single "Willow". Described as a "sister record" to Folklore.
